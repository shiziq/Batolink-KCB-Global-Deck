## SLIDE 1: CINEMATIC HERO — "KCB Has the Infrastructure. We Unlock the Revenue."
**Visual:** Full-screen city skyline at dusk with slow Ken Burns zoom. Gold gradient overlay from left.
**Duration:** 2.5 minutes

**Opening:**
Stand center-left to not block the cityscape. Let the image breathe for 5 seconds before speaking.

**Script:**
"Good morning, Directors. Before I say a word, look at this city. Every building is a business. Every street is a transaction waiting to happen. KCB is already here — 47 branches, 1.3 million agents, 34 million customers."

Pause. Gesture to the skyline.

"But here's what we discovered: having the infrastructure is not the same as monetizing it. Batolink is the bridge that converts KCB's incredible physical and digital assets into daily, frictionless, cashless revenue."

**Emphasize:** This is not a vendor pitch. This is a strategic partnership between equals.

## ANTICIPATED QUESTIONS & RESPONSES

**Q: "These projections seem aggressive."**
A: "These are actually conservative. We based them on 15% adoption in year one, not the 40-50% we've seen in comparable fintech launches. The KSh 2.4B assumes only Nairobi-level density. Rural expansion could double it."

**Q: "What about regulatory approval from CBK?"**
A: "Batolink operates as a technology layer, not a deposit-taking institution. All funds sit in KCB accounts. CBK oversight flows through KCB's existing license. We've had preliminary discussions and they view this as a bank-led innovation."

**Q: "M-Pesa will see this as competition."**
A: "M-Pesa is a wallet. We are the bridge from wallet to physical world. In fact, M-Pesa users are our best prospects — they have money in their phones and nowhere physical to spend it. Safaricom gets transaction volume. KCB gets the banking relationship. Both win."

**Q: "What if the pilot fails?"**
A: "Then we part as friends after three months, having spent only our own capital. The risk is entirely on Batolink. That's how confident we are in the model."

**Q: "Can this scale beyond Kenya?"**
A: "The technology is country-agnostic. Tanzania, Uganda, Rwanda, DRC — all markets where KCB already has presence. The Kenyan pilot becomes the proof case for regional expansion."
